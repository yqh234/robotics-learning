Paper 1

FoundationPose: Unified 6D Pose Estimation and Tracking of Novel Objects (2026-06-20)

Links: arXiv · DOI：无

Open PDF Paper Library Chinese Guide arXiv

6D poseRGB-DperceptionYCB-Video

Why It Was Chosen

Chosen because the library had a classic instance-level RGB-D pose paper, but still lacked a representative novel-object and foundation-assisted 6D pose system.

Core Methods

  • One unified framework for model-based and few-reference model-free pose estimation
  • Neural implicit representation for novel-view synthesis across settings
  • LLM-assisted large-scale synthetic data generation for training pose estimation and tracking

Robotics Insight

Its key contribution is turning novel-object 6D pose from a bag of special-case tricks into a more unified platform capability.

Main Limitation

It still depends on fairly strong 3D priors such as CAD models or a few reference views, so it is not yet a full open-world or category-level solution.

Paper 2

Dex-Net 2.0: Deep Learning to Plan Robust Grasps with Synthetic Point Clouds and Analytic Grasp Metrics (2026-06-20)

Links: arXiv · DOI：10.15607/RSS.2017.XIII.058

Open PDF Paper Library Chinese Guide arXiv

grasping6-DoF graspclutterdepth

Why It Was Chosen

Paired with FoundationPose because one tackles novel-object pose estimation while the other anchors robust grasp scoring from depth, forming a clearer perception-to-grasp chain.

Core Methods

  • Analytically labels large synthetic point-cloud grasp datasets with robust grasp metrics
  • Trains a GQ-CNN to predict parallel-jaw grasp success directly from depth images
  • Validates that synthetic-only training transfers to a real ABB YuMi grasp planner

Robotics Insight

Its enduring contribution is reframing grasp planning as large-scale synthetic-data generation plus learned grasp scoring, a template that influenced much of later sim-to-real grasping.

Main Limitation

It is mainly a parallel-jaw grasp scorer for simpler settings and does not cover the full complexity of cluttered 6-DoF or multi-finger task-aware grasping.

Comparison

FoundationPose is about making novel-object 6D pose estimation and tracking more unified and reusable.

Dex-Net 2.0 is about making grasp planning fast and scalable through synthetic data and learned grasp scoring.

Read them in that order to connect generalized pose perception with a classic grasp-planning backbone.

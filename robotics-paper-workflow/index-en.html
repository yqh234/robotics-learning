Paper 1

PoseCNN: A Convolutional Neural Network for 6D Object Pose Estimation in Cluttered Scenes (2026-06-22)

Links: arXiv · DOI: 10.15607/RSS.2018.XIV.019

Open PDF Paper Library View Analysis arXiv

6D poseRGBYCB-Videoclutter

Why It Was Chosen

The library already had stronger later pose systems, but it still lacked the milestone paper that defined the known-object RGB pose pipeline and introduced YCB-Video.

Core Methods

  • Decouples translation into image-center localization plus depth prediction, and regresses rotation with quaternions.
  • Introduces a symmetry-aware loss to stabilize training on objects with ambiguous orientations.
  • Releases the YCB-Video dataset, which became a standard benchmark substrate for later 6D pose research.

Robotics Insight

Its robotics contribution is turning pre-grasp object pose estimation into a trainable end-to-end perception module that explicitly handles clutter, occlusion, and symmetry.

Main Limitation

It assumes known object instances, not category-level or open-world pose, and depth mainly enters as refinement rather than an integrated representation.

Paper 2

AnyGrasp: Robust and Efficient Grasp Perception in Spatial and Temporal Domains (2026-06-22)

Links: arXiv · DOI: 10.1109/TRO.2023.3281153

Open PDF Paper Library View Analysis arXiv

graspingtemporalreal robotthroughput

Why It Was Chosen

The library already had grasp planning, grasp generation, and the GraspNet benchmark, but it still lacked the paper that best bridges those ideas into a high-throughput deployed grasping system.

Core Methods

  • Uses dense supervision across both spatial and temporal domains, mixing real perception with analytic labels.
  • Adds center-of-mass awareness to favor stable grasps rather than merely reachable ones.
  • Tracks grasp correspondences across observations to support dynamic grasping with strong real-system throughput.

Robotics Insight

Its key robotics value is reframing grasp perception as a temporally consistent, stability-aware, deployment-minded system rather than a one-shot detector.

Main Limitation

It is still centered on parallel-jaw grasping and does not fully solve dexterous-hand, semantic, or contact-rich grasp execution.

Comparison

PoseCNN is about making known-object pose estimation in clutter trainable, standard, and useful to downstream manipulation.

AnyGrasp is about making grasp perception continuous, stable, and fast enough for real deployment rather than isolated evaluation.

Read them in that order to connect perception infrastructure with deployment-minded grasp execution.

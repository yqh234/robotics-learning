Today's theme: both papers ask whether robotic models are physically trustworthy. A model output should not merely look plausible; it should be executable by a robot and remain valid in real or near-real physical settings.

Paper 1

DexSim2Real: Foundation Model-Guided Sim-to-Real Transfer for Generalizable Dexterous Manipulation (2026-06-15)

Paper link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05241

Open PDFPaper LibraryChinese TranslationarXiv

Why It Is Recommended

This paper focuses on transferring dexterous manipulation policies from simulation to real robots. It places a vision-language foundation model inside the sim-to-real training loop, uses it to guide domain randomization, and combines tactile-visual cross-attention with a progressive skill curriculum.

Core Methods

  • Foundation-model-guided domain randomization
  • Tactile and visual policy fusion
  • Progressive skill curriculum for contact-rich manipulation

Robotics Innovation I See

The key value is not simply “using a foundation model for robotics.” It puts the foundation model into the calibration stage of sim-to-real transfer, allowing the model to judge whether the simulated world is close enough to the real world. This shifts robot training from tuning only the controller to jointly tuning the environment, perception, and policy.

Main Limitation

The experiments remain concentrated on a limited set of dexterous manipulation scenarios. More evidence is needed for open environments, long-horizon tasks, and cross-platform generalization.

Paper 2

RoboWM-Bench: A Benchmark for Evaluating World Models in Robotic Manipulation (2026-06-15)

Paper link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19092

Open PDFPaper LibraryChinese TranslationarXiv

world modelembodied evaluationmanipulation planningvideo-to-control

Why It Is Recommended

This paper proposes an important evaluation perspective: a generated manipulation video that looks reasonable is not necessarily executable by a robot. RoboWM-Bench asks whether the generated manipulation process is physically executable.

Core Methods

  • Convert human or robot manipulation videos into embodied action sequences
  • Validate task completion in physics simulation
  • Separate visual plausibility from embodied executability

Robotics Innovation I See

It moves world-model evaluation from “does the result look right?” to “can the generated process be executed by a robot?” This shift matters because robotic intelligence ultimately lives in action, contact, and physical constraints. RoboWM-Bench connects visual generation, action recovery, and physical validation into a more realistic evaluation loop for robotic foundation models.

Main Limitation

It mainly provides a benchmark and diagnostic framework rather than a deployable control policy.

Comparison

DexSim2Real is a system-building paper: it aims to transfer policies to real dexterous robots.

RoboWM-Bench is an evaluation paper: it asks whether generated world-model behavior is truly executable.

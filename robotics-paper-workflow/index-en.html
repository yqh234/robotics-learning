Paper 1

DenseFusion: 6D Object Pose Estimation by Iterative Dense Fusion (2026-06-19)

Links: arXiv · DOI：10.1109/CVPR.2019.00346

Open PDF Paper Library Chinese Guide arXiv

6D poseRGB-DperceptionYCB-Video

Why It Was Chosen

Chosen because the library still lacked a durable RGB-D 6D pose-estimation anchor paper.

Core Methods

  • Separate RGB and point-cloud encoders with pixel-wise dense fusion
  • End-to-end iterative pose refinement
  • Instance-level 6D pose estimation on YCB-Video and LineMOD

Robotics Insight

Its real contribution is not just using RGB plus depth, but making them interact inside one dense, refinable inference loop that behaves like a deployable perception module.

Main Limitation

It assumes known object instances and usually depends on upstream detection or segmentation.

Paper 2

Contact-GraspNet: Efficient 6-DoF Grasp Generation in Cluttered Scenes (2026-06-19)

Links: arXiv · DOI：10.1109/ICRA48506.2021.9561877

Open PDF Paper Library Chinese Guide arXiv

grasping6-DoF graspclutterdepth

Why It Was Chosen

Paired with DenseFusion because together they form a practical chain from pose-aware perception to grasp proposal generation.

Core Methods

  • Treats scene points as potential contacts
  • Reduces 6-DoF grasping to a learnable contact-based parameterization
  • Predicts grasp distributions directly instead of a long sequential pipeline

Robotics Insight

Its lasting effect is shifting grasping from discrete grasp search toward directly learned grasp distributions grounded in visible contact geometry.

Main Limitation

It depends heavily on depth quality and often needs object filtering for target-driven grasping.

Comparison

DenseFusion is about estimating object pose reliably from RGB-D.

Contact-GraspNet is about generating feasible 6-DoF grasp proposals from cluttered depth observations.

Read them in that order to connect object-centric perception with clutter-aware grasp execution.

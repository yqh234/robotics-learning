DexSim2Real: Foundation Model-Guided Sim-to-Real Transfer for Generalizable Dexterous Manipulation

A sim-to-real system for real-world dexterous manipulation, focusing on how foundation models can assist domain randomization and tactile-visual fusion.

Summary: This paper addresses how a dexterous-hand policy learned in simulation can transfer more reliably to a real robot. It uses a vision-language model to assess whether simulated visuals are close to real scenes and adjusts domain randomization accordingly. It is most useful for readers interested in real robot deployment, dexterous control, sim-to-real gaps, and multimodal perception fusion.

RoboWM-Bench: A Benchmark for Evaluating World Models in Robotic Manipulation

A benchmark for robotic world models, emphasizing whether generated videos carry physically executable meaning for robots.

Summary: This paper is less about a new controller and more about a new evaluation ruler. A manipulation video generated by a world model may look plausible, but that does not mean a robot can execute it. RoboWM-Bench converts generated manipulation processes into embodied action sequences and checks physical task completion in simulation.
